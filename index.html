About

Standing at the threshold of life and death—where the stakes could not be higher and the meaning could not be deeper.

My journey to medicine began in Ladakh, a cold desert where geopolitical tension is heavy but medical access is scarce. There, in a medical camp at 14,000 feet, I watched a woman's lips turn the color of a bruised plum as High Altitude Cerebral Edema set in. That night, a Lama walked fifteen miles through freezing mountain passes with a failing heart, trusting nothing but our camp's reputation.

These experiences taught me that a physician stands outside ordinary hierarchies, trusted with something more precious than any payment—the threshold between existence and permanence.

Philosophy

Saving the life in front of you is not enough—you must build structures that prevent suffering before it arrives.

Dr. Tsering Norbu, the first surgeon of Ladakh, taught me this lesson. Rather than waiting for children with Rheumatic Heart Disease to walk miles with failing hearts, he traveled village to village with prophylactic penicillin. The solution wasn't in the operating theater—it was in prevention.

This philosophy shapes my work: combining rigorous bench science with systems thinking, moving from conference room strategy to bedside impact.

Clinical Cardiology & AI/ML

June 2022 – Sept 2025

Designed core triage algorithm for AI-powered chatbot monitoring post-PCI patients. The system stratified symptoms into severity tiers using patient-reported data and vital signs.

Impact: Clinical trial (n=164, 6-month follow-up) reduced unplanned cardiovascular hospitalizations from 16.3% to 4.9%. 80% of patients reported decreased post-procedure anxiety.

But the technology revealed its limits: user retention was low in rural areas. The algorithm worked, but the human element was missing. This taught me that innovation without adoption is incomplete.
